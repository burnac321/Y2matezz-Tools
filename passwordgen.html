Password Security Education

Use Strong Passwords
A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using personal information like birthdays or names.
Don't Reuse Passwords
Use unique passwords for each account. If one account gets compromised, reused passwords can lead to multiple account breaches.
Use a Password Manager
Password managers securely store all your passwords and can generate strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts.
Enable Two-Factor Authentication
2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification beyond just your password.
Change Passwords Regularly
Update important passwords every 3-6 months, especially for sensitive accounts like email, banking, and social media.
Avoid Common Password Mistakes
Don't use sequential numbers/letters, common dictionary words, or personal information that can be easily guessed.
